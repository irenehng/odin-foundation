Sushi Bake

Description

My housemate introduced me to this dish, which was all over TikTok, and I've been obsessed with this since! I can hardly wait to get it out of the oven every time I make it. Definitely a satisfying meal that I count on to tide me over until the next time I get sushi.

Serves: 2 people

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups of imitation crab, shredded
  • 1 big fillet of salmon
  • 1 whole avocado (or more if you prefer, I usually go with 2 as an avocado fan)
  • 1.5 cups of rice
  • Seaweed (both the nori kind or loasted lavers kind is good)
  • Optional: Any additional seafood (I've made it with scallops and shrimps as well)

Seasoning & Garnish

  • kewpie mayo
  • srriracha or any type of hot sauce you have
  • furikake
  • sesame oil
  • soy sauce
  • any seasoning you like for the salmon: I use chili powder, salt, pepper, garlic powder

Steps

  1. Cook the rice in a rice cooker or a pot (look up instructions for this!)
  2. Meanwhile, season and air fry/ pan fry/ bake the salmon. I usually air fry the salmon at 400 degrees F or 180 degrees C for 8-10 minutes, flipping halfway. If you have other seafood you want to use, lightly stir fry them on a pan with the same seasoning until lightly cooked
  3. Preheat the oven to 350-375 degrees F. Line a baking tray with parchment paper or aluminium foil. When the rice is done cooking, scoop it out into a bowl, mix with sesame oil and furikake, and line the tray with the rice mix.
  4. In a bowl mix your cooked salmon, shredded imitation crab and seafood together with soysauce, kewpie mayo and srriracha to taste. Mash the salmon up into a mushed texture. Put this mixture on top of the rice
  5. You can add avocado and seaweed on top if you prefer. Put the tray into the oven to bake for 20 minutes
  6. After it's baked, drizzle more srriracha and mayo on top, and enjoy the bake by scooping some into pieces of seaweed to serve as a wrap!
